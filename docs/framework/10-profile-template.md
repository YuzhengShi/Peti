## Writing Guidelines

### For "Who They Are"
This section is the heart of the profile. It should:
1. **Integrate across all domains** — don't restate each domain separately
2. **Be written as a person, not a report** — warm, specific, observational
3. **Lead with strengths** — what's working, what's interesting about this person
4. **Acknowledge tensions** — if their profile shows competing patterns (e.g., high openness + high neuroticism), name the tension as a real part of who they are
5. **Be specific enough to be useful** — Peti needs to know HOW to show up for this specific person

### For Domain Sections
Each domain narrative should follow the feedback template from 03-feedback-language.md:
1. What the pattern looks like for this specific person
2. How it shows up in daily life
3. Context and variability — this isn't permanent
4. Connection to other domains where relevant

### For "What this means for Peti"
These sections are Peti's operational guide:
1. **Be actionable** — tell Peti what to DO, not just what the pattern is
2. **Be specific to this person's combination** — don't just restate the band
3. **Consider cross-domain interactions** — connected patterns change what Peti should do

### Overall Quality Standard
The user answered 106 questions across 6 domains covering personality traits, attachment,
personality functioning, sleep, emotion regulation, and daily functioning. The generated
profile must reflect the depth and breadth of that data. Every domain should have
substantive narrative. Band labels alone are not sufficient — they are the starting point,
not the end product.
