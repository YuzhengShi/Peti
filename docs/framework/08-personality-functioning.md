# Domain: Personality Functioning

> Source: Framework Section 9
> Layer: 2 (Modifiable Regulatory System)
> Timeframe: General patterns (no specific timeframe)
> Items: 12 (DSM-5 AMPD Criterion A / LPFS-BF 2.0 framework)
> Response scale: 5-point (Strongly disagree → Strongly agree)

## What This Domain Measures

Personality functioning refers to the coherence and adaptive capacity of self and interpersonal processes. It captures how coherently and adaptively individuals organize their sense of self and relationships — NOT diagnosing personality pathology.

Based on DSM-5 Alternative Model for Personality Disorders (AMPD) Criterion A, which conceptualizes personality functioning as a dimensional continuum from healthy adaptive functioning to severe impairment. This assessment focuses on the **non-pathological end** of that continuum.

Sharp and Wall describe it as capturing "the contribution humans make as agentic authors to the interpretation and management of the self."

## Subscales (4 Domains → 2 Higher-Order)

### Self-Functioning

| Subscale | Items | What It Captures |
|----------|-------|-----------------|
| **identity** | 1-3 | Clear, stable sense of self; consistency across situations; realistic understanding of strengths and limitations |
| **self-direction** | 4-6 | Meaningful goals that guide decisions; ability to reflect and learn from experience; sense of direction in life |

### Interpersonal Functioning

| Subscale | Items | What It Captures |
|----------|-------|-----------------|
| **empathy** | 7-9 | Understanding others' perspectives; recognizing how actions affect others; appreciating different experiences |
| **intimacy** | 10-12 | Forming close meaningful connections; mutual care in relationships; maintaining closeness over time |

**Higher-order aggregation:**
- Self-Functioning = Identity + Self-Direction
- Interpersonal Functioning = Empathy + Intimacy

## How to Interpret Each Band — By Subscale

### Identity
- **Lower**: "Your responses suggest that your sense of who you are may feel uncertain or shifting right now. You might find that how you see yourself changes depending on the situation or who you're with. This is a common experience, especially during transitions or periods of change, and identity coherence can develop over time."
- **Moderate**: "Your responses suggest a generally stable sense of who you are, though it may waver at times. You have some understanding of your strengths and limitations, and your self-concept is reasonably consistent across situations."
- **Higher**: "Your responses suggest a clear and stable sense of who you are. You seem to have a realistic understanding of your strengths and limitations, and your sense of self stays fairly consistent across different situations and contexts."

### Self-Direction
- **Lower**: "Your responses suggest that finding direction or meaningful goals has been challenging. You may feel uncertain about what you're working toward, or find it hard to use internal standards to guide your decisions. This can shift as life circumstances change and new purposes emerge."
- **Moderate**: "Your responses suggest you have some sense of direction — meaningful goals exist, though they may not always feel clear or motivating. You're generally able to reflect on your experiences, though self-direction may feel easier at some times than others."
- **Higher**: "Your responses suggest a clear sense of direction. You tend to have meaningful goals that guide your decisions, and you're able to reflect productively on your behavior and learn from your experiences."

### Empathy
- **Lower**: "Your responses suggest that understanding others' perspectives has been harder recently. You may sometimes miss how your actions affect others, or find it difficult to appreciate that others experience things differently. This is a skill that can develop with awareness and practice."
- **Moderate**: "Your responses suggest a reasonable capacity for empathy — you can often understand where others are coming from, though it may take effort in some situations."
- **Higher**: "Your responses suggest strong empathic capacity. You tend to understand others' perspectives, recognize how your actions affect them, and appreciate that people can experience the same situation very differently."

### Intimacy
- **Lower**: "Your responses suggest that forming or maintaining close connections has been challenging. You may find it difficult to establish the depth of connection you want, or to sustain closeness over time. This can reflect current circumstances as much as personal patterns, and can shift with new relationship experiences."
- **Moderate**: "Your responses suggest a mixed picture with close relationships — you're capable of meaningful connections, though maintaining consistent closeness may sometimes be challenging."
- **Higher**: "Your responses suggest a strong capacity for close, mutual relationships. You tend to form meaningful connections and maintain them with mutual care and understanding over time."

## Interpretation Framework Rules

1. **Patterns of organization, not pathology**: Emphasize how coherently individuals organize self and relationships — NOT diagnosing dysfunction
2. **Dimensional and developmental**: Personality functioning exists on a continuum and can develop over time
3. **Distinct from personality traits**: This assesses "how personality is organized" rather than "what personality traits you have"
4. **Context for other patterns**: Position personality functioning as providing context for understanding emotion regulation, attachment, and functioning patterns
5. **Non-diagnostic**: EXPLICITLY state this is NOT assessing personality disorders

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Personality functioning ↔ Attachment**: Both involve how you experience yourself in relationships. Personality functioning = overall coherence of self/interpersonal processes; attachment = specific closeness/distance tendencies. They overlap but capture different aspects.
- **Personality functioning → Daily functioning**: Rossi & Diaz-Batanero found "self-functioning predicted functional impairment along and beyond personality domains." Lower personality functioning often shows up in daily life.
- **Identity ↔ Neuroticism**: Emotional instability can make identity coherence harder. If both are notable, name the connection.
- **Self-direction ↔ Conscientiousness**: Goal pursuit and discipline overlap. Higher conscientiousness often co-occurs with stronger self-direction.

## Modifiability

**Moderately modifiable.** "These patterns can develop and strengthen over time with self-reflection, new relationship experiences, and sometimes therapy or coaching." More stable than emotion regulation or sleep, but clearly developmental — people grow in personality functioning throughout adulthood.
