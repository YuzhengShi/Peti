# Domain: Daily Functioning

> Source: Framework Section 5
> Layer: 3 (Outcome Anchor)
> Timeframe: Past 30 days
> Items: 12 (WHODAS 2.0 / ICF framework)
> Response scale: 5-point (None / Mild / Moderate / Severe / Extreme)

## What This Domain Measures

Daily functioning measures real-world activity and participation across multiple life domains. It is the **outcome anchor** of the assessment — not who you are, but how life has been going recently.

Conceptualized according to the WHO International Classification of Functioning (ICF), which defines functioning as distinct from both health conditions and personal characteristics. The ICF emphasizes "function, not the health condition" and "categorizes the situation, not the person."

## Subscales (6 ICF Domains)

| Subscale | Items | What It Captures |
|----------|-------|-----------------|
| **cognition** | 1-2 | Concentrating on tasks, remembering important things |
| **mobility** | 3-4 | Standing/physical activity for extended periods, getting around |
| **self-care** | 5 | Personal hygiene (washing, dressing, grooming) |
| **getting-along** | 6-7 | Dealing with strangers, maintaining friendships/close relationships |
| **life-activities** | 8-10 | Household responsibilities, work/school, getting everything done |
| **participation** | 11-12 | Social activities/community events, dealing with unexpected challenges |

## How to Interpret Each Band

### Lower (less difficulty)
The user reports little to no difficulty across most areas of daily life over the past month. Things have been flowing relatively well.

**Frame as**: "Over the past month, your daily life seems to have been flowing well across most areas. You've been able to manage your responsibilities, engage socially, and handle what comes up without significant difficulty."

### Moderate
Some areas are going smoothly while others present mild-to-moderate challenge. This is extremely common and reflects normal variation in how life goes.

**Frame as**: "Your responses suggest a mixed picture — some areas of daily life have been going smoothly, while others have required more effort recently. This is a very common pattern, especially when life is busy or stressful."

### Higher (more difficulty)
The user reports notable difficulty in multiple areas of daily functioning. This is a state, not a trait — it reflects current circumstances.

**Frame as**: "Your responses suggest that several areas of daily life have been harder than usual over the past month. This could reflect a particularly demanding period — stress, health, sleep, and life circumstances all affect how easily daily tasks get done."

## Interpretation Framework Rules

1. **Current state, not identity**: "How life has been going recently" — NOT "your level of disability" or "your capability"
2. **Modifiable and contextual**: Explicitly note that functioning is influenced by sleep, stress, support, health, and life circumstances
3. **Normalization**: Everyone experiences periods of greater and lesser difficulty
4. **Strength-based**: Highlight areas of relative ease alongside areas of difficulty
5. **Profile, not total**: Present as a profile across domains — user may have ease in mobility but difficulty in getting-along, for example

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Sleep → Functioning**: Poor sleep often directly impacts daily functioning. If both are lower, name this connection.
- **Emotion regulation → Functioning**: Difficulty managing emotions can affect concentration, relationships, and task completion.
- **Neuroticism → Functioning**: Higher emotional reactivity may make daily challenges feel more overwhelming.
- **Attachment → Getting-along**: Relationship patterns (anxiety or avoidance) may show up specifically in the getting-along subscale.

## Modifiability

**Highly modifiable.** "Daily functioning often improves when underlying factors (sleep, stress, health, support) are addressed." This is the most responsive domain to change — frame it as a snapshot that can shift quickly.
