# Domain: Sleep-Wake Regulation

> Source: Framework Section 6
> Layer: 2 (Modifiable Regulatory System)
> Timeframe: Past 7 days
> Items: 8 (PROMIS Sleep Disturbance framework)
> Response scale: 5-point (Not at all / A little bit / Somewhat / Quite a bit / Very much)
> Exception: Item 1 uses (Very poor / Poor / Fair / Good / Very good)

## What This Domain Measures

Sleep-wake regulation is a **biological regulatory system** that bidirectionally influences and is influenced by psychological functioning, emotion regulation, and daily activities. Unlike personality traits, sleep quality is a modifiable physiological process that serves as both an indicator of current well-being and a potential intervention target.

The framework prioritizes **sleep quality over sleep duration** based on evidence that sleep quality shows stronger and more consistent associations with mental health and functioning outcomes than sleep duration alone (Seow et al., N=6,126; Um et al., N=225,915).

## Subscales (3 Facets)

| Subscale | Items | What It Captures |
|----------|-------|-----------------|
| **quality** | 1-3 | Sleep quality rating, refreshingness, satisfaction with sleep |
| **continuity** | 4-6 | Difficulty falling asleep, trouble staying asleep, restless/interrupted sleep |
| **daytime-impact** | 7-8 | Daytime tiredness/fatigue, fatigue interfering with daily activities |

**Scoring note**: For quality subscale, higher scores = better sleep. For continuity and daytime-impact, higher scores = more difficulty (reverse-direction). The stored band already accounts for this — "lower" means poorer sleep support, "higher" means better sleep support.

## How to Interpret Each Band

### Lower (strained/disrupted sleep)
Sleep has been less restorative recently. The user may be experiencing difficulty falling or staying asleep, unrefreshing sleep, or daytime fatigue that interferes with activities.

**Frame as**: "Your responses suggest that sleep hasn't been very supportive recently. You may have been experiencing restless or unrefreshing sleep, difficulty falling or staying asleep, or tiredness that carries into your day. This is a common experience, especially during stressful periods, and sleep patterns often improve with changes to habits, stress management, or addressing what's disrupting rest."

### Moderate
Sleep has been adequate but not consistently restorative. Some nights are better than others.

**Frame as**: "Your sleep over the past week seems to have been fair — not consistently restful, but not severely disrupted either. You may notice some variation night to night, which is normal. Sleep quality naturally fluctuates with stress, schedule changes, and life circumstances."

### Higher (well-supported sleep)
Sleep has been restorative, consistent, and not significantly impacting daytime functioning.

**Frame as**: "Your responses suggest that sleep has been generally supportive recently — restorative, reasonably consistent, and not leaving you drained during the day. This is a good foundation, as quality sleep supports mood, concentration, and how you handle what comes up."

## Interpretation Framework Rules

1. **Current state, not trait**: "How your sleep has been recently" — NOT "your sleep quality as a person"
2. **Bidirectional influence**: Sleep both affects and is affected by stress, mood, schedule, and health
3. **Modifiable**: Sleep quality can improve with changes in routine, stress management, or support
4. **Contextual**: Sleep naturally varies with life circumstances
5. **Non-diagnostic**: This is NOT a sleep disorder assessment

## Cross-Domain Connections

- **Sleep → Emotion regulation**: Sleep quality predicts baseline emotion and emotion regulation capacity. Poor sleep makes emotional management harder.
- **Sleep → Daily functioning**: Sleep directly affects concentration, energy, and ability to perform daily tasks. If both are lower, they're likely connected.
- **Neuroticism → Sleep**: Higher neuroticism is associated with poorer sleep quality (meta-analytic r = 0.287). Name this connection if both patterns appear.
- **Sleep → Everything**: Sleep is a biological foundation. When sleep is strained, it can cascade into multiple other domains.

## Modifiability

**Highly modifiable.** "Sleep patterns often improve with changes to sleep habits, stress management, and addressing underlying factors affecting rest." Frame as a support system that can be strengthened, not a permanent characteristic.
